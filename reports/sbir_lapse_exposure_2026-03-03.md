**Date:** March 03, 2026

**PROPRIETARY & CONFIDENTIAL**

---

## Executive Summary

On October 1, 2025, SBIR/STTR program authorizations lapsed. The FY2026 NDAA, signed December 18, 2025, did not reauthorize the program. As of March 03, 2026, 5 months into the lapse, Aperture Signals has identified 2,378 defense technology companies with active SBIR pipelines and limited alternative funding. These companies hold a combined $11,191.2M in SBIR awards across 10 federal agencies. 2,355 companies have reached Phase II -- indicating validated technology that was on track for transition to production. 96% have raised no private capital, making them entirely dependent on government R&D funding that is now disrupted.

## Methodology

This report is based on Aperture Signals' cross-reference of SBIR.gov award data, SEC EDGAR Form D filings, and USASpending.gov contract records. Companies are flagged as exposed when SBIR awards represent more than 70% of their total government funding and they have raised less than $1M in private capital. SBIR data includes awards through Q4 2025 (pre-lapse). Private capital data is sourced from SEC Form D filings, which capture Regulation D exemptions but may undercount total fundraising for companies that raised capital through other structures.
